Digesting Zomato's Line Chart

Picture a captivating Zomato dashboard, where a mesmerising line chart brings to life the diverse culinary landscape of different cities. This creative visualisation intertwines the count of cuisines and the average cost for two people, uncovering insightful connections and fostering a deeper understanding of dining preferences.

The peaks and valleys of the lines signify the popularity of specific cuisines within different regions. This insight allows us to identify the unique culinary preferences of each city's residents, providing valuable information for restaurants and entrepreneurs looking to tailor their offerings to cater to local tastes and preferences.

The line chart's peaks and troughs reflect not only the count of cuisines but also the average cost for two people. Cities with a higher count of cuisines but relatively lower average costs may signify an abundance of budget-friendly dining options, making it an attractive destination for food enthusiasts on a budget.

Map: Mapping Flavors

Insights from Zomato's Stacked Bar Chart

The stacked bar chart showcases a spectrum of colours representing different average price ranges, creating a delightful visual palette. The varying heights of the bars symbolise the distribution of restaurants across different price brackets, from affordable eateries to upscale dining establishments.

By examining the chart, we observe patterns of colour within each stacked bar, indicating the presence or absence of online delivery for each restaurant. This insight uncovers the value proposition of restaurants that strike a balance between affordability and convenience, appealing to customers seeking quality meals at reasonable prices, delivered right to their doorstep.

It allows us to identify niche segments, such as high-end restaurants with online delivery options or budget-friendly eateries that focus on dine-in experiences. This insight helps restaurateurs and entrepreneurs understand their target audience, tailor their marketing strategies, and optimise their operations to cater to specific customer segments.
